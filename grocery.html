1.Shop Locally

Buying seasonal produce ensures you get the freshest and most flavorful ingredients. Seasonal fruits and vegetables are often more affordable and packed with nutrients.

2.Buy in Small Quantities

Fresh groceries have a shorter shelf life, so it’s best to buy in small quantities that you can use within a week. This helps avoid waste and ensures you're eating the freshest ingredients

3.Choose organic when possible

Organic fruits and vegetables are grown without synthetic pesticides and fertilizers, which can benefit both your health and the environment. While organic can be more expensive, you can prioritize buying organic for the ‘Dirty Dozen’ list—produce most likely to carry pesticide residue

4.Consider Farmers Markets

Shopping at farmers markets not only supports local farmers but also guarantees you get fresh, often organic, and seasonal produce. Farmers markets often offer a wider variety of less common fruits and vegetables.

5.Choose Whole Grains Over Processed Grains

Organic fruits and vegetables are grown without synthetic pesticides and fertilizers, which can benefit both your health and the environment.
